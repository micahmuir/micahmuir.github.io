Animal Head Camera

AKA, my throw-it-on-the-wall-an-see-what-sticks version.

Things are expensive.

Luckily, I had a number of components that were already-kind-of-sort of around the scale I was looking to build for.

  • Noctua Fan from a 3D printer build I never ended up using would be a low-current, quiet way of cooling the internal raspberry pi
  • Raspberry Pi 5 - I wanted two camera operating at the same time, and the raspberry pi 5 platform is the most user friendly, affordable way to get 2 CSI cameras working simultaneously
  • 5V Regulator. I wanted to be able to hook this guy up to a range of power sources so I wouldn’t be limited in the field.
  • Neopixel rings for fun. If these are supposed to mimic eyes, I wanted the thing to be able to indicate status, or otherwise provide quick signaling for vision applications

Hardware Build

Below is the rough CAD model I made to size components. As this was experimental and I wanted to get this printing as fast as possible, I didn’t bother to include the standardized fasteners and offsets, although I acknowledge at least one of these times that’ll come back to bite me.

Inspired by my larger SFFPC build, I entertained the notion of this as a Super SFFPC. At the end of the day, most of the parts had their analogue in desktop PC building.

Not shown between these two points was a whole of (oh no that’s not going to work) until I had printed two copies of the box and smashed them together as I found spare
