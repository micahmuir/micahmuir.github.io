Motivation

A year and a half ago, I had a vision of two dancers on a darkened stage, moving together in a call-response fashion. When moving synchronously, beams of light flared out across space to connect them, but only when they mirrored each other within a certain threshold. I didn’t know how I was going to achieve this exactly, but I knew it wasn’t impossible.

Requirements

This project was borne out of need for a low cost, high-ish performance network camera solution for my interactive spatial projects. I needed a generalized method and hardware to locate a human in 3D space and perform pose estimation.

It needed to do the following:

  • Provide real-time depth imaging alongside simultaneous “regular” imaging
  • Be relatively small
  • Send imaging data across CAT5/6 network cables as fast as I possible can.
  • Leverage a much more powerful central computer to perform GPU calculation
