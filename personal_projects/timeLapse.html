Timelapse Camera

I'd been curious about adding "board-level camera" to my maker toolbox for quite some time. I finally picked up both global-shutter and rolling-shutter camera modules for the Raspberry Pi, and grabbed a Pi 5 so I could use its two CSI ports. The goal: a flexible multi-camera system with Python tooling for capture and assembly into week-long timelapses.

My goal was a turnkey system where I set an interval, leave for a week, and comeback to an automatically generated timelapse. One of the tricky bits was triggering the ringlight to flash when light levels were low enough to keep recording overnight, but without leaving a bright light on all night. I ended up taking two photos - one at low res to judge the ambient illumination of the scene and estimate the lux level, and the other at high res for the final timelapse.

Multi-camera Raspberry Pi setup with automated lighting control for week-long timelapse photography.
