Terrain Coasters

Christmas Gifts 2024

Last year for Christmas, my friends and family did a “diy” Christmas, where the idea was to spend less than $20 per gift, and ideally have them be handmade.

I happen to have a lot of DIY tools, and wanted to use 3D printing, but in a way that delivered more quality and thoughtfulness than those 3D printed dragons you see all the time at craft fairs.

I wanted to make something useable, durable, and at least somewhat interesting to look at.

Local Terrain

I used https://www.map2stl.com/ to grab STL topo models of places near and dear to the people I was making the gifts for, which ended up being several mountains and nature preserves in the PnW.
I found 100 mm glass discs that I wanted to be the cup contact surface. The problem remained of how to assemble it all.

I didn’t want to use any fasteners, because while I might have unlimited small metric bolts, my family doesn’t, and I didn’t want them send them replacement hardware overtime. A thread 100 mm in OD, with a pitch large enough to get threads and a helix angle that wouldn’t self-undo wasn’t feasable with this aspect ratio and FDM printing.

So, I designed a twist lock mechanism that, coupled with the layer lines, provided more than enough friction to sandwich the pieces together, and a year later, it seems like they’re holding up fine.
