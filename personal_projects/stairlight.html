Companion Stairlight

This project was born of frustration. Our very old, strangely wired house has the stairwell light switch at the bottom of the stairs. Late at night, that meant walking down in the dark, turning on the light, doing the thing, then turning it off and heading back up in darkness again. Usually with full arms, which meant putting everything down just to flip a switch. It was a pain, so I built a stair light that follows you automatically.

A TFL03 ToF sensor measures distance along the stair axis—immune to ambient light. Mounted on a 1/4-20 arm and aimed up the hallway.
TFL03 serial distance data is bridged by an Arduino and broadcast over NRF24L01+ so future projects can subscribe.
Low-profile printed enclosure for the controller—mounted with putty to avoid drilling in a rental.
Inside: Teensy 4.1 for headroom, network IO, and scalability. ~300 LEDs under the stairs in the current setup. I really need to learn basic PCB design.
