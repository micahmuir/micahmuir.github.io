3D Printed Vacuum Gripper

I designed, tested, and deployed a multi-cup, multi-zone vacuum gripper EOAT to securely pick and place a variety of package sizes and weights in an automated warehouse. The tool is almost entirely 3D printable on a consumer-grade FDM printer with a specialized slicing profile. This slicing profile enabled complex internal geometry for a vacuum manifold to be quickly produced, tested, iterated, and improved upon based on real-world performance feedback.

Features

  • Unibody manifold design. Almost every feature is built into the main body print, and all additional pieces can be printed in under 20 hours on a single buildplate
  • Multi-zone vacuum triggering ability, enabling efficient suction power across a range of box sizes
  • Tremendous vacuum sealing performance for a very low cost
  • Significantly more lightweight than a metal analogue → increased end effector payload capacity
  • Continuous onboard sensor feedback to a larger robotic control system
