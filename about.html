Micah Muir Thomas

I exist at the intersection of many worlds. I was born in South Africa and grew up in Oregon, Singapore, Scotland, and California. My heritage reflects a mix of cultures— my mother is Caucasian, my father was half Black and half Japanese, and my grandmother was unconstitutionally interned at Heart Mountain during WWII.

In school, I was “the artistic kid,” but I was equally driven by a deep scientific curiosity about how both I and the world worked. I earned a degree in Mechanical Engineering with a minor in Studio Art from Santa Clara University. Since then, I’ve worked in robotics, product design, and art.

I am currently a mechanical engineer at Lab0, developing a robotic full-system solution for unpacking, transporting, and palletizing inbound product. My projects have ranged from autonomous vision systems, to large-scale robotic structures, to interactive art installations. Across all of these, my curiosity and eclectic interests allow my work and art to inform one another.

I learned to code not as an end in itself, but as a way to leverage engineering to create a kind of magic. Years of frugal innovation have taught me to redefine what’s possible with limited resources, blending practicality with imagination.

You can see more of the smaller projects I keep busy with in the Personal Projects section.
